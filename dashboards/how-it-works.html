üìä How Risk Scores Are Calculated

1. Mental Health Score (35% weight)

Inputs:

  • Stress level (1-5 scale)
  • Academic pressure (1-5 scale)
  • Daily mood selection
  • Historical stress patterns

How it works:

Starts at 100 (perfect). Points deducted for:

  • High stress (level 4-5): -15 to -60 points
  • High academic pressure: -10 to -40 points
  • Negative moods (sad/stressed): -30 to -40 points
  • Sustained high stress (7+ days): additional -10 points

2. Lifestyle Score (25% weight)

Inputs:

  • Sleep hours per night
  • Sleep consistency over time

Optimal range: 7-9 hours

Penalties:

  • Less than 6 hours: -30 points
  • 6-7 hours: -15 points
  • More than 9 hours: -10 points
  • Inconsistent sleep pattern: additional -10 points

3. Digital Wellbeing Score (20% weight)

Inputs:

  • Daily screen time hours
  • Screen time trends

Recommended limit: 4 hours or less

Penalties:

  • More than 8 hours: -40 points
  • 6-8 hours: -25 points
  • 4-6 hours: -10 points
  • Increasing trend: additional -10 points

4. Physical Health Score (20% weight)

Inputs:

  • Physical activity days per week
  • Exercise consistency

WHO recommendation: 5+ days per week

Penalties:

  • Zero activity: -40 points
  • 1-2 days: -25 points
  • 3-4 days: -10 points
  • Chronic inactivity: additional -15 points

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Is this system diagnosing mental health conditions?

No. This is a wellbeing tracking and early warning system, not a diagnostic tool. It helps identify students who might benefit from support, but only qualified healthcare professionals can provide diagnoses.

Can I fake my check-ins to get better scores?

While you can input any data, the system is designed to help you, not judge you. Honest check-ins provide better personalized support. The credits system also validates consistency to prevent gaming.

Will my parents see all my data?

Only if you consent. Parents receive positive, supportive notifications (like "Your child is maintaining healthy habits!"), never raw check-in data. Critical alerts require your explicit consent.

What happens if I press the SOS button?

Immediate alert to your counselor and emergency contacts. You'll see helpline numbers instantly. This is for genuine emergencies - mental health crises, urgent support needs, or feeling unsafe.

How accurate is the AI?

The system uses evidence-based thresholds (WHO sleep guidelines, research on screen time, etc.). It's designed for early detection, not precision diagnosis. Think of it as a smoke detector, not a fire investigation.
